24-Pin ATX (Main Power)

24-PIN ATX
  • Where: Motherboard (largest connector)
  • Purpose: Main power for entire motherboard
  • Look: Big rectangular block, 20+4 configuration
  • Always required!

4/8-Pin EPS (CPU Power)

4/8-PIN EPS
  • Where: Near CPU socket (top of motherboard)
  • Purpose: Dedicated power for CPU
  • Look: Square 4-pin or 4+4 (8-pin)
  • Variants: 4-pin, 4+4, 8-pin

6/8-Pin PCIe Power (GPU)

6/8-PIN PCIe
  • Where: Graphics card edge
  • Purpose: Extra power for GPU
  • Look: 6-pin or 6+2 (8-pin)
  • High-end cards: Need 2√ó or 3√ó connectors
‚ö†Ô∏è DON'T CONFUSE!
  • EPS (CPU) ‚â† PCIe Power (GPU)
  • Both look similar but go to different places
  • EPS ‚Üí Top of motherboard near CPU
  • PCIe Power ‚Üí Side of graphics card

SATA Power

SATA POWER
  • Where: SATA drives, SSDs, HDDs
  • Look: Flat L-shaped connector (15-pin)
  • From: PSU directly to drive

üîß QUICK TROUBLESHOOTING TIPS

No Power / Won't Boot

Check in order:
  1. Is PSU switch on? (back of PSU)
  2. Is power cable plugged in?
  3. 24-pin ATX connected to motherboard?
  4. 4/8-pin EPS connected near CPU?
  5. F_PANEL (power button) connected?
  6. RAM seated properly?

Storage Not Detected

SATA Drive:
  • ‚úÖ SATA data cable connected to motherboard?
  • ‚úÖ SATA power cable from PSU connected?
  • ‚úÖ Try different SATA port on motherboard
M.2 Drive:
  • ‚úÖ Fully inserted and screw tight?
  • ‚úÖ Right M.2 slot? (some boards have SATA-only M.2 slots)
  • ‚úÖ Check BIOS - M.2 slot might share lanes with SATA ports

USB Device Not Working

  • Try different USB port
  • Try USB 2.0 port (more compatible)
  • Check if F_USB header connected (front ports)
  • Power-only cable? Try different cable

GPU Not Detected

  • ‚úÖ Fully seated in PCIe x16 slot?
  • ‚úÖ 6/8-pin PCIe power connected?
  • ‚úÖ Monitor cable in GPU, not motherboard?
  • ‚úÖ Check BIOS - iGPU might be priority
